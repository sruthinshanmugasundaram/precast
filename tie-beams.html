TVK’s Foundation Elements

In precast construction systems, elements are cast in the factory under strict quality supervision using state-of-the-art machinery. The finished elements are transported to the site and erected efficiently.

Foundation Types

Foundation for these buildings will be based on the nature of the soil:

  • Pile Foundation
  • Open Foundation

Footing

Based on the nature of the soil, the type of foundation is decided — either Pile & Pile Cap or Open Foundation. Suitable for poor soil areas where open footings are not possible.

Grade of Foundation Concrete: M25

Pedestal

The top level of the pedestal is decided based on the building’s finished floor level. It is precast with foundation bolts or dowel bars for stable support.

Grade of Concrete: M40

Tie Beam

Tie beams connect the columns and are pre-stressed, spanning between columns (8–9m). They also act as retaining walls between two foundations.

Grade of Concrete: M40

FOUNDATION - PROCEDURE

Pile Foundation

  1. Boring for Pile
  2. Concrete Filling (Placing of reinforcement cage & concreting, then placing of precast pile cap)
  3. Pedestal Erection
  4. Placing of Precast Tie Beam

Open Foundation

  1. Excavation
  2. Placing of Precast Footing
  3. Pedestal Erection
  4. Placing of Precast Tie Beam

Material Specifications

  • Precast Footing: M40
  • Precast Pedestal: M40
  • Precast Tie Beam: M40

Design Based On:

  • IS: 6006 – 1983 (Uncoated Stress Relieved Strand for Pre-Stressed Concrete)
  • IS: 1343 – 1980 (Tensile Stress, Elastic Deformation, Safety Factors)

Design Considerations:

  • Foundation – Soil Condition & Wind Load
  • Precast Pedestal – Soil Condition & Wind Load
  • Precast Tie Beam – Soil Condition & Wall Load
